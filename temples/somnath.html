Somnath Temple

Revered as the first among the twelve Jyotirlingas of Shiva.

History

Somnath has been destroyed and rebuilt many times across its long history. The present temple was reconstructed in 1951 in the Maru-Gurjara (Chalukya) style, an effort spearheaded by Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, and was dedicated by then-President Rajendra Prasad.

Architecture

Maru-Gurjara (Chalukya) style in sandstone, with an intricately carved shikhara rising above the sanctum on the Arabian Sea shore.

Legend & tradition

Tradition holds that the moon god Soma built the original shrine in gold to be cured of a curse, giving the site its name 'Somnath' (Lord of the Moon).

Darshan & timings

Generally open early morning to night, with aarti three times daily. Confirm exact timings with the temple before visiting.

How to reach

Nearest railway station: Veraval (~7 km). Nearest airports: Diu and Rajkot.
