Omkareshwar Temple

Set on the sacred island of Mandhata in the Narmada river, said to be shaped like the symbol 'Om'.

Primary deity
Shiva (Jyotirlinga)
Location
Mandhata, Khandwa district, Madhya Pradesh
Category
Jyotirlinga
Coordinates
22.245, 76.151
View on map →

History

A historic pilgrimage centre on the Narmada, the site has temples and ghats developed over many centuries.

Architecture

North Indian Nagara style stone temple with a tall shikhara, reached across the Narmada.

Legend & tradition

Tradition links the island's Om-like shape to the sanctity of the site.

Festivals

Maha ShivaratriKartik PurnimaNarmada Jayanti

Darshan & timings

Generally open from early morning to night with regular aartis. Confirm exact timings with the temple before visiting.

How to reach

Nearest railway station: Omkareshwar Road / Indore. Nearest airport: Indore (~77 km).
