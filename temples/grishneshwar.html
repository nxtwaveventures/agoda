History

The present temple was rebuilt in the 18th century by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar in red volcanic rock.

Architecture

South-Indian-influenced stone temple with finely carved pillars and a layered shikhara.

Legend & tradition

Named after the devotee Kusuma (Ghushma), whose unwavering devotion is said to have brought Shiva to manifest here.

Festivals

Maha ShivaratriKartik Purnima

Darshan & timings

Generally open from early morning to night with regular aartis. Confirm exact timings with the temple before visiting.

How to reach

Nearest railway station: Aurangabad (Chhatrapati Sambhajinagar). Nearest airport: Aurangabad (~30 km).
