Kashi Vishwanath Temple

One of the most revered Shiva shrines, on the west bank of the Ganga in the ancient city of Kashi.

Primary deity

History

The present temple was built in 1780 by Maharani Ahilyabai Holkar of Indore; its spire was later gold-plated with a donation from Maharaja Ranjit Singh. The Kashi Vishwanath Dham corridor connecting the temple to the Ganga ghats was inaugurated in 2021.

Architecture

Compact Nagara-style temple crowned by a gold-plated shikhara and dome.

Legend & tradition

Kashi is traditionally held to be the eternal city of Shiva, who is said to grant liberation (moksha) to those who pass away here.

Darshan & timings

Generally open from pre-dawn mangala aarti until late night with several aartis. Confirm exact timings and darshan rules with the temple.

How to reach

Nearest railway station: Varanasi Junction. Nearest airport: Lal Bahadur Shastri International Airport, Varanasi.
